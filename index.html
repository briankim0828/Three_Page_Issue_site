FILTER BUBBLES

In the 21st century, everybody is connected to the internet. It doesn't matter if you are a male, female, old, young, rich or poor: our lives have become inseparable with the web. The vast amounts of information we consume on a daily basis is unprecedented, as we never had such easy access before in our history. While this could have been the best method for our species to overcome each other's differences and understand one another better, we are unfortunately under the unfluence of filter bubbles: our own digital rabbithole of information created by recommendation algorithms. Such algorithms make use of our digital data to track our behaviors, preferences, and opinions to hand-select information that it deems we would enjoy most. Because of this, we are increasingly limiting ourselves to smaller pools of information, such as similar preferences, similar political opinions, and much more. We are becoming more isolated than ever before.

The technology that was expected to endorse discussion, became a place of separation, as every single experience online became unique between every user.
